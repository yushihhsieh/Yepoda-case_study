Executive Summary

1. Key findings
   
        - Total marketing spend: approximately $8.1 million across six paid channels (Paid Search, Paid Social, TV, Display, Affiliate and Email).
        - Total transactions: 408,170, driven by 139,851 new customers, suggesting strong acquisition volume and conversion throughput.
        - ROI performance in Q1–Q2 of 2024 remains solid but lower than 2023, indicating potential market saturation or increased costs.
        - End-of-Year Recovery: Q4 shows a gradual rebound, validating the impact of seasonal promotions.


2. Channel performance rankings with supporting metrics
   
       - Paid Search leads with a total revenue of $11.53M, followed by Paid Social at $9.60M, and TV at $7.64M.
       - Efficiency across all channels is remarkably consistent, ranging from 4.746 to 4.750.
       - Email shows the highest efficiency (4.750), indicating that for each dollar spent, it generates the highest return relative to spend.
       - Paid Social and Display also show strong efficiency values above 4.74, demonstrating effective allocation of budget.

3. Budget optimization recommendations with expected impact 

        - The Email channel currently represents only 4.8% of total spend but delivers the highest ROAS (600.09). Recommended share increases to 40.9%.
        - The Paid Search channel has low ROAS (99.42), recommended to cut the share of total spend from 28.65% to 6.7%. 

4. Data quality issues or limitations discovered 

        - Missing 2023-10-29 and 2024-10-27 data in revenue, marketing_spend and external_factors tables.
        - Different granularity in revenue and marketing_spend tables, leading to not possible to join two dataset in channel level.

5. Suggested next steps for deeper analysis 

        - Continue investing in high revenue channels like Paid Search and Paid Social to maintain revenue growth.
        - Reallocate budget toward Email and Affiliate channels, which demonstrate superior ROAS and scalability.
        - Concentrate Budgets in high ROI Months (Jan–Apr); reduce investment in low season (Jul–Oct)
        - Implement performance monitoring which track ROI monthly and compare against seasonality indices to improve predictive budgeting.
